<Section icon={Swords} title="Managing Mid-Air Chaos & Hazards">

The cabin of the aircraft is a breeding ground for unexpected emergencies. Players must deal with loose cargo, dangerous wildlife, and hostile passengers who seek to sabotage the flight. Managing these hazards quickly is essential to prevent catastrophic structural damage.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Wildlife Cargo">

    - **Disposal**: Grab the animals and throw them out of the open cabin doors.

  </Card>
  <Card title="Hostile Passengers">
    - **Armed Threats**: Some passengers may draw weapons mid-flight.
    - **Neutralization**: Use melee attacks or firearms to disarm them quickly.

  </Card>
  <Card title="External Attacks">

    - **Defense**: Use onboard defensive weapons to return fire.
    - **Danger Level**: Structural damage leading to rapid decompression.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

When dealing with cargo like loose animals or explosive materials, players must open the cabin doors mid-flight to discard them. This action introduces decompression risks, pulling unsecured objects and players toward the exit. Timing these actions during stable flight conditions is crucial for crew safety.

| Hazard | Immediate Action | Tools Needed |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| Cabin Fire | Spray the flames until extinguished | Fire Extinguisher |
| Loose Alligator | Grab and throw out of the cabin door | None (Manual Grab) |
| Armed Passenger | Disarm and neutralize immediately | Assault Rifle / Melee |
| Hull Breach | Patch the leak to stop decompression | Repair Kit |

<Callout type="warning" title="Extreme Hazards">
Discarding dangerous items through open doors is effective, but players must secure themselves first. Standing too close to an open door during a sharp turn can result in falling out of the plane.
</Callout>

To complete a successful flight, the crew must establish a reliable routine. Following a structured set of steps helps manage the escalation of chaotic events from takeoff to landing.

  <Step num="1" title="Pre-Flight Coordination">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Cabin Monitoring">

  <Step num="3" title="Threat Response">

During high-stress moments, prioritizing tasks prevents the crew from becoming overwhelmed. A single unaddressed fire can quickly spread and destroy the engines, leading to an uncontrollable crash.

| Flight Phase | Primary Threat | Crew Priority |
|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| Takeoff | Engine instability | Monitoring system gauges |
| Mid-Flight | Armed hijackers and cargo leaks | Defense and hazard containment |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Co-Op Strategies">

Mastering the game requires advanced coordination and efficient resource management. Crews that communicate effectively can handle multiple crises simultaneously without losing control of the aircraft.

Using the cabin layout to your advantage is key. Keep critical tools like fire extinguishers and weapons in central locations where any crew member can reach them quickly. Avoid hoarding items in the cockpit, as this leaves the rest of the cabin defenseless during sudden breaches.
