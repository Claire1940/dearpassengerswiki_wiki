<Section icon={Target} title="Passenger Types and Risk-Reward Analysis">

Not all passengers are created equal. In **Dear Passengers**, your manifest determines the frequency of "incidents" during the flight. While standard travelers just want a drink and a nap, high-paying VIPs or unruly individuals will test your team's patience and reaction speed. Understanding the risk profile of your cabin is the first step toward a profitable career.

| Passenger Category | Known Behavior | Risk Level | Main Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | Expects food/drinks | Low | Service delays cause minor frustration |
| **Hard-to-Please** | Constant demands | Medium | Distracts crew from critical emergencies |
| **Unruly** | Disruptive/Aggressive | High | Can start fights or interfere with crew |
| **VIP** | Extremely demanding | High | High payout but zero tolerance for errors |

Managing these groups requires a dedicated **Passenger Service** role. If you ignore a thirsty passenger for too long, their frustration adds "Cabin Pressure," a hidden metric that makes the aircraft more prone to internal chaos.

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Mixing **Unruly Passengers** with **Illegal Cargo** is an "Extreme" risk strategy. One distraction can lead to a total loss of the aircraft.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Step-by-Step Emergency Response">

When the alarm sounds, panic is your greatest enemy. Whether it’s a bird strike or an escaped crocodile, following a standardized response protocol will save your flight.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Assess and Communicate">
    Identify the source of the problem (Fire, Cargo, or Passenger) and alert the Pilot immediately so they can stabilize the aircraft.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Contain the Immediate Threat">
    Use extinguishers for fires or restraints for unruly passengers. If cargo has escaped, clear all standard passengers from the immediate vicinity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stabilize the Cabin">
    Once the threat is neutralized, secure loose luggage and reseat passengers. The physics engine in **Dear Passengers** means a messy cabin leads to more injuries during the next turbulence event.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resume Service">
    Return to food and drink delivery to lower the Cabin Pressure and restore passenger satisfaction levels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Emergency Fact">
Engine damage from bird strikes often leads to secondary cabin fires. Always check the rear of the plane after a loud impact.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Cargo Management: Handling Dangerous Goods">

Cargo is often more profitable than passengers but significantly more dangerous. From illegal shipments to literal wild animals, your cargo hold is a ticking time bomb.

| Cargo Type | Hazard Level | Recommended Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Regular Freight** | Low | Check straps after turbulence |
| **Fragile Goods** | Medium | Pilot must avoid sharp maneuvers |
| **Illegal Cargo** | High | Keep hidden from suspicious passengers |
| **Animal (Crocodile)** | Extreme | Treat containment failure as Priority 1 |

Live cargo, like the crocodiles shown in official FLEXUS footage, requires a crew member to stay within earshot of the cargo hold. If you hear scratching or hissing, it’s time to check the crates.
