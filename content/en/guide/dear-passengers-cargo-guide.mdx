<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Cargo Securing and Flight Plan">

Succeeding in your transport missions requires a systematic approach to cabin organization and flight execution. Follow this step-by-step procedure to ensure your cargo reaches the destination intact.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Flight Manifest">
    Review the cargo choices before takeoff. Balance high-paying, high-risk items with standard, low-maintenance passengers to keep the overall cabin chaos manageable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assign the Cargo Lead">
    Designate one cabin crew member to monitor the cargo hold. This player must check container integrity and secure loose luggage before the pilot initiates sharp turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Establish Pilot Communication">
    The pilot must warn the cabin crew before entering dynamic weather or navigating air pockets. This warning allows the cargo lead to hold down unstable crates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Mid-Flight Shifts">
    If turbulence strikes, immediately pause routine food service. Clear the main aisles of loose bags and debris to maintain pathways to the cargo bay.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Contain Breaches Immediately">
    If live cargo escapes (such as the crocodiles shown in official gameplay footage), treat it as a priority one emergency. Contain the threat before it damages the hull or injures passengers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Operational Impact">
Keeping cabin walkways clear is not just about passenger comfort. Blocked aisles delay your emergency response times, turning a simple loose crate into a flight-ending disaster.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Managing Cargo Emergencies and Flight Hazards">

When things go wrong in the cabin, they go wrong quickly. The physics-based simulation in Dear Passengers means that one unaddressed hazard can rapidly trigger secondary emergencies. For example, a minor bird strike can damage an engine, causing the pilot to lose altitude control, which then throws unsecured cargo out of its hold, starting a cabin fire.

| Emergency Scenario | First-Response Role | Primary Action Required | Escalation Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Turbulence Shift** | Cabin Crew | Secure loose items and lock cargo latches | Blocked aisles, injured passengers |
| **Engine Fire** | Nearest Crew | Extinguish source; pilot stabilizes aircraft | Loss of flight control, hull breach |
| **Cargo Escape** | Cargo Handler | Clear passengers, redirect/contain animal | Damaged systems, passenger panic |
| **External Attack** | Gunner Crew | Man external weapons to defend hull | Structural failure, total decompression |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Never send your entire cabin crew to handle an external threat or repair a hull breach. Leaving the passenger cabin and cargo hold completely unattended allows minor shifts to escalate into uncontrollable fires or escapes.
</Callout>
