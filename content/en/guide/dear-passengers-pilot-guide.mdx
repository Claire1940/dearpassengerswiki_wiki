<Section icon={Shield} title="Passenger and Cargo Risk Management">

The economy of Dear Passengers revolves around risk versus reward. Before taking off, you must evaluate the load you are carrying. Some passengers are "Hard-to-Please," requiring constant attention, while some cargo is "Extreme Risk," such as live animals that can break out of their crates.

| Category | Risk Level | Expected Reward | Primary Hazard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Passengers** | Low | Baseline | Minor service complaints |
| **Unruly Passengers** | High | High | Physical disruption, interference |
| **Regular Cargo** | Low | Baseline | Shifting weight during turns |
| **Illegal Cargo** | High | Very High | High attention required, risky |
| **Animal Cargo** | Extreme | Premium | Escaped predators in the cabin |

Managing these risks requires a proactive approach. Don't wait for a crocodile to escape before checking the cargo hold. Regular inspections are mandatory for high-tier payouts.

<Callout type="info" title="Management Note">
Loose luggage and cabin debris are "Medium" risks that don't provide extra pay but can block aisles and injure crew during turbulence. Keep the floors clear!
</Callout>

The physics engine in Dear Passengers ensures that every incident has a tangible impact. If a bird strike occurs, the engine might catch fire, and the plane will pull in that direction. The crew must work together to solve these problems before they escalate into a total loss.
