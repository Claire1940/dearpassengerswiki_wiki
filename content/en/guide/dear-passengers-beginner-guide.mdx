Standard passengers provide a steady, low-risk income, while specialized "Hard-to-Please" or "Unruly" passengers offer significantly higher rewards at the cost of constant attention and potential disruptions.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Co-op Roles and Team Strategy">

Effective teamwork is the backbone of any successful flight in Dear Passengers. While players can technically move anywhere, assigning specific roles prevents "emergency paralysis" where everyone tries to fix the same problem while ignoring others. In a standard crew, the division of labor should be clearly defined before takeoff.

Communication is the most powerful tool in your arsenal. The Pilot must act as the "eyes" of the crew, spotting weather patterns and bird strikes before they impact the aircraft. Meanwhile, the Cabin Lead must filter information to the Pilot, only reporting critical emergencies that might require a change in flight path, such as a fire or structural hull damage.

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Strategy">
In two-player sessions, the Pilot should handle navigation and external repairs, while the second player manages both service and cargo. Avoid "Extreme" risk loads until you have a full four-player crew.

<Section icon={Shield} title="In-Flight Hazard Management">

Once the wheels are up, the environment becomes dynamic. Hazards in Dear Passengers are not just scripted events; they are systemic failures that can escalate if not handled quickly. A minor bird strike can damage an engine, which leads to a fire, which then panics the passengers, creating a chain reaction of disasters.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identification">
    Recognize the hazard early (e.g., the sound of a crate breaking or the visual of an engine smoking).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="First Response">
    The nearest crew member must drop routine tasks (like serving drinks) to address the immediate threat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Containment">
    Use extinguishers for fires or physical restraints for unruly passengers to stop the problem from spreading.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Stabilization">
    The Pilot stabilizes the aircraft to ensure the cabin crew can move safely through the aisles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Recovery">
    Clean up debris, reseat passengers, and repair any minor hull damage before the next wave of chaos.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Hazard | First Responder | Escalation Risk | Recommended Action |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Turbulence** | Pilot | High | Level the wings, warn cabin |
| **Engine Fire** | Cabin Crew | Critical | Use extinguisher, Pilot cuts fuel |
| **Animal Escape** | Cargo Warden | Extreme | Clear aisles, use containment |
| **Hull Breach** | Support Crew | Critical | External repair, oxygen masks |
| **Bird Strike** | Pilot | Medium | Check engine health, adjust pitch |

<Callout type="info" title="Operational Priority">
Always prioritize "Aviation" (flying the plane) over "Service." A happy passenger on a crashing plane still results in a "Game Over."
</Callout>
