<Section icon={Target} title="Passenger Management & Service Excellence">

Passengers in Dear Passengers are not just passive NPCs; they are dynamic entities with needs, moods, and the ability to cause physical obstructions. Managing their expectations while the plane is tilting at a 45-degree angle is the core challenge of the cabin crew.

| Passenger Type | Risk Level | Behavior Pattern | Response Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | Low | Expects basic food/drink service. | Routine service during calm air. |
| **Demanding** | Medium | Frequent requests/high impatience. | Prioritize their orders to avoid noise. |
| **Unruly** | High | Disrupts cabin/interferes with crew. | Isolate or restrain if safety is at risk. |
| **VIP** | Medium | Requires high-quality service. | Assign dedicated crew for max payout. |

To maintain a high rating, follow this standard service loop:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Galley Preparation">
    Prepare food trays and drink containers in the galley. Ensure all items are placed on the service cart and the cart is locked to the floor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Service Delivery">
    Move the cart slowly through the aisle. Physics can cause the cart to crush passengers or crew if the pilot makes sudden turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Conflict Resolution">
    Address any passengers with "!" icons above their heads. Neglecting them increases cabin stress levels and decreases final rewards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Waste Management">
    Collect empty trays and trash. Loose items in the cabin become dangerous projectiles during turbulence or air pockets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Never leave a service cart unlocked in the aisle. A single air pocket can turn a cart into a lethal weapon that damages the aircraft hull or knocks out crew members.
</Callout>

<Section icon={AlertTriangle} title="Survival During Mid-Air Disasters">

When things go wrong in Dear Passengers, they go wrong quickly. A bird strike can lead to an engine fire, which causes a loss of altitude, which then triggers cabin panic. The cabin crew must remain calm and follow established emergency protocols to ensure the flight reaches its destination.

**Emergency Priority List:**
1. **Aircraft Integrity**: Repair hull breaches and extinguish fires.
2. **Crew Safety**: Ensure at least one person is conscious and mobile.
3. **Passenger Protection**: Keep passengers in their seats during turbulence.
4. **Cargo Preservation**: Secure high-value items from sliding out of breaches.
5. **Routine Service**: Completely suspend food/drink service until the crisis is over.

| Emergency | First Responder | Success Criteria |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine Failure** | Pilot | Successful emergency landing/restart. |
| **Bird Strike** | Cabin Crew | Hull repaired/fire extinguished. |
| **Severe Turbulence** | Entire Team | All objects secured/no crew injuries. |
| **Unruly Riot** | Cabin Lead | Order restored/passengers seated. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Expert Advice">
Use the internal communication system to tell the pilot when the cabin is "Clean" (all objects secured). Only then should the pilot attempt high-G maneuvers to avoid storms or external threats.
</Callout>
