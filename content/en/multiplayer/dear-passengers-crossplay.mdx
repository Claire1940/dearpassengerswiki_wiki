<Section icon={Target} title="How to Set Up Co-op Lobbies in Dear Passengers">

Setting up a multiplayer session is straightforward. Follow these steps to prepare your aircraft, gather your crew, and coordinate your roles before taking off into turbulent skies.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Create or Join a Lobby">
    Launch the game and navigate to the multiplayer menu. Choose to host a new private flight lobby or search for an open lobby hosted by your Steam friends.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Passengers and Cargo">
    Before starting the flight loop, review the available passenger manifests and cargo orders. Higher-paying contracts introduce more difficult passengers and hazardous cargo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assign Crew Roles">
    Delegate roles among your party. Ensure at least one player is designated as the Pilot, while the remaining players prepare for cabin service and cargo protection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Initiate the Flight">
    Once all players confirm they are ready in the lobby, the host can start the match to begin the flight.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Lobby Optimization Tip">
For your first few flights, choose low-risk passenger and cargo combinations. This allows your crew to master the basic movement physics and interaction controls before handling extreme hazards.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Co-op Role Strategy and Team Coordination">

To survive the escalating mid-air disasters in Dear Passengers, your crew must divide and conquer. Running around aimlessly will quickly lead to crashed planes, escaped animals, or angry passengers.

| Role | Primary Location | Key Responsibilities | Emergency Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pilot** | Cockpit | Fly aircraft, navigate weather, stabilize turbulence | Aircraft control, warning cabin |
| **Cabin Lead** | Passenger Cabin | Coordinate services, prioritize tasks, relay info to pilot | Fire control, structural breaches |
| **Passenger Service** | Passenger Seating | Serve food and drinks, placate demanding flyers | Unruly passenger containment |
| **Cargo Handler** | Cargo Hold | Secure shifting crates, monitor hazardous materials | Escaped live cargo retrieval |

<Callout type="info" title="Role Flexibility">
While having assigned roles keeps the operation organized, players must remain flexible. If a fire breaks out in the cabin, the nearest crew member should immediately abandon routine service to extinguish it.
</Callout>
