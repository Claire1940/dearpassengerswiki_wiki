<Section icon={Users} title="Multiplayer Roles and Responsibilities">

In **dear passengers co op**, success depends on how well players divide their labor. While the game allows for free-form movement, establishing dedicated roles before takeoff significantly increases your survival rate. The aircraft requires constant attention, from the flight deck to the rear loading ramp. If everyone tries to fly the plane at once, the cargo will inevitably be lost to the wind or predators.

| Role | Primary Responsibility | Key Equipment | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Pilot** | Maintaining altitude and navigation | Flight Yoke, Radar | Medium |
| **The Loader** | Securing cargo and managing weight | Grapples, Straps | High |
| **The Defender** | Fending off hijackers and rivals | AK-47, Flare Gun | Very High |
| **The Engineer** | Repairing engines and hull leaks | Wrench, Welder | Medium |

Establishing these roles early prevents the "everyone is a pilot" syndrome, which usually ends with the plane nose-diving while an alligator eats the remaining crew. Each player must be prepared to leave their post if a localized emergency—like a nuke countdown—requires immediate attention.

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Never leave the cockpit unattended while the rear cargo door is open. The change in cabin pressure can pull unanchored players out of the aircraft, leading to an immediate game over for that crew member.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Survival Strategies">

As you progress in **dear passengers co op**, the missions become significantly more complex. You will face "Friendship Testing" scenarios where the game forces players to make split-second decisions that could sacrifice one player for the safety of the plane. Advanced teams use specific maneuvers to maximize their efficiency and profit.

**The Jettison Maneuver**
If the plane is too heavy to gain altitude, the loader must quickly identify the lowest-value cargo and dump it. This requires timing; dumping cargo over a populated area can result in penalties, while dumping over the ocean is safe.

**External Repair Tactics**
In extreme cases, the engines may catch fire. One player might need to climb out onto the wing while the plane is in motion. This is extremely dangerous and should only be attempted while the pilot maintains a steady, low-speed flight path.

| Strategy | Difficulty | Requirement | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wing Walking** | Very High | Tether Rope | Engine Repair |
| **Synchronized Drop** | Medium | Voice Chat | Precision Delivery |
| **Emergency Glide** | High | Pilot Skill | Fuel Conservation |

<Callout type="warning" title="Hazard Warning">
Standing on the wing without a tether is a 90% fatality risk. Always ensure the "Loader" has secured a rope to the fuselage before any player attempts external repairs.
</Callout>
