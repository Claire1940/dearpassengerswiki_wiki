<Section icon={AlertTriangle} title="Managing Mid-Air Emergencies">

Disaster is not a possibility in this game; it is a certainty. The physics engine ensures that every flight will eventually face a crisis that requires immediate intervention. Whether it is an external threat or an internal failure, the crew must react within seconds.

**Common Flight Hazards:**
- **Bird Strikes**: Causes immediate engine damage and hull breaches.
- **Engine Fire**: Requires a cabin crew member to leave their post to extinguish the flames.
- **Dynamic Turbulence**: Throws everything not bolted down—including players—across the cabin.
- **Unruly Passengers**: Some passengers may become aggressive or refuse to stay seated during emergencies.

| Hazard | First Responder | Priority | Required Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hull Breach** | Repair Crew | 1 (Critical) | Seal the leak/Repair exterior |
| **Escaped Animal** | Cargo/Security | 2 (High) | Contain and re-secure crate |
| **Passenger Conflict** | Service Lead | 3 (Medium) | De-escalate or restrain |
| **Service Spill** | Any Cabin Crew | 4 (Low) | Clear aisles to prevent tripping |

<Callout type="warning" title="Emergency Protocol">
If a hull breach occurs, the Pilot must immediately descend to a lower altitude. Failure to do so will cause cabin decompression, making it nearly impossible for the crew to move or perform repairs.
</Callout>

</Section>
