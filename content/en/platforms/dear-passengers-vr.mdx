<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Completing Your First Flight">

Success in **Dear Passengers VR** requires more than just keeping the plane in the air. Every journey is a delicate balance between profit and survival. Beginners should focus on understanding the flight loop, which transitions from pre-flight planning to post-turbulence recovery. 

The primary objective is to deliver your manifest—both human and cargo—to the destination with minimal damage. Because the game uses a physics-driven engine, every sharp turn by the pilot or unlatched suitcase in the cabin has real consequences for the safety of the flight.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Manifest Selection">
    Before takeoff, you must choose your passengers and cargo. Higher payouts come with "Hard-to-Please" passengers or "Illegal Cargo," which increase the likelihood of mid-flight incidents.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Role Assignment">
    Divide your team. At least one person must be in the cockpit at all times, while the rest of the crew manages the cabin. Communication is the only way to survive sudden air pockets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Service and Security">
    Once in the air, the cabin crew must serve food and drinks to keep passenger satisfaction high. Simultaneously, you must check that cargo remains strapped down during maneuvers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Emergency Response">
    When disasters strike—such as engine fires or escaped animals—suspend all service immediately. Use fire extinguishers or restraints to contain the threat before it compromises the hull.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Physics Warning">
Loose objects become projectiles during turbulence. Always ensure carts and luggage are returned to their designated slots before the pilot enters a storm zone.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Activity} title="Flight Hazards and Emergency Protocols">

The environment in **Dear Passengers VR** is your greatest enemy. Dynamic weather can turn a clear flight into a nightmare, while mechanical failures require immediate hands-on repairs. Understanding the hierarchy of hazards is vital for long-term progression.

**Common Hazards and Responses:**
- **Dynamic Weather**: The pilot must adjust altitude and speed. Cabin crew should secure all loose carts.
- **Turbulence**: Physics will toss players and items. Stay crouched or hold onto fixed structures to avoid injury.
- **Bird Strikes**: Can cause engine failure or hull breaches. Requires a crew member to exit the cabin for external repairs.
- **Onboard Fires**: Spreads quickly through the cabin. Requires immediate use of extinguishers and passenger relocation.

| Hazard Type | First Responder | Critical Action | Escalation Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine Fire** | Pilot/Tech | Cut fuel/Extinguish | Total loss of control |
| **Hull Breach** | Emergency Tech | External repair | Decompression/Passenger loss |
| **Cargo Escape** | Cargo Warden | Containment/Restraint | Crew injuries/Service stop |
| **Unruly Mob** | Cabin Lead | De-escalation | Cabin-wide chaos |

<Callout type="warning" title="Hull Integrity">
If a hull breach occurs, the pilot must descend to a safe altitude immediately. Failure to do so will result in rapid passenger health depletion.
</Callout>
