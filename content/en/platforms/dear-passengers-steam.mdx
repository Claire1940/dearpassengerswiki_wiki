<Section icon={Target} title="How to Complete a Flight: Step-by-Step Beginner Guide">

Success in Dear Passengers requires more than just keeping the plane in the air. Every journey is a delicate balance between maximizing profit and minimizing the risk of total aircraft destruction. Beginners should focus on mastering the basic flight loop before attempting high-reward, high-risk contracts that involve dangerous cargo or unruly passengers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Contract">
    Before takeoff, you must choose your loadout. This includes selecting the number of passengers and the type of cargo. Higher payouts come with greater risks, such as aggressive passengers or fragile, hazardous materials.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assign Crew Roles">
    Communication is vital. One player must stay in the cockpit to pilot the plane, while the rest of the team manages the cabin. Decide who will handle passenger service and who will monitor the cargo hold.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Cabin Service">
    Once airborne, the cabin crew must serve food and drinks to keep passenger satisfaction high. Happy passengers are less likely to cause trouble during turbulence or delays.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Respond to Hazards">
    As the pilot navigates weather and air pockets, the cabin crew must secure loose objects. If a fire breaks out or cargo escapes, the team must react instantly to prevent an escalation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Deliver and Collect">
    Land the plane at the destination with the majority of your cargo and passengers intact. Your final payout is determined by the safety and satisfaction levels maintained throughout the flight.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Chaos Management">
The physics engine means that every sharp turn by the pilot affects the cabin crew. Always communicate before performing maneuvers to avoid throwing your teammates into the ceiling.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Flight Hazards and Emergency Response">

The "physics-based" nature of **Dear Passengers Steam** means that disasters are dynamic. A simple air pocket can send a meal trolley flying, which might then strike a passenger or break a cargo crate. Players must be prepared to switch from routine service to emergency response in seconds.

**Common Emergency Scenarios:**
- **Dynamic Weather**: Storms increase the frequency of turbulence, making movement in the cabin nearly impossible.
- **Engine Damage**: Often caused by bird strikes, this requires the pilot to maintain control while the cabin crew prepares for potential hull breaches.
- **Onboard Fires**: Can start in the galley or cargo hold. If not extinguished quickly, they can spread to the seating area.
- **External Attacks**: Some scenarios involve defending the aircraft from external threats using mounted equipment.

| Hazard | First Responder | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Turbulence** | Pilot | Stabilize altitude and warn the crew |
| **Fire** | Nearest Crew | Use extinguisher and move passengers |
| **Engine Failure** | Pilot | Adjust flight path for emergency landing |
| **Escaped Animal** | Cabin Crew | Use containment tools to secure cargo |

<Callout type="warning" title="Hull Integrity">
If the aircraft hull is damaged, the cabin will depressurize. Crew members must prioritize repairs or oxygen management over all other tasks to survive.
</Callout>

</Section>
