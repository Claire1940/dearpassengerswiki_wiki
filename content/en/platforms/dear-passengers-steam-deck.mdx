<Section icon={Gamepad2} title="Optimizing Controls for Handheld Airline Management">

Managing an entire aircraft on a 7-inch screen requires a precise control setup. **Dear Passengers** involves complex interactions, from toggling cockpit switches to restraining aggressive passengers. The Steam Deck's trackpads are particularly useful for the Pilot role, allowing for fine-tuned adjustments to the flight path that traditional joysticks might struggle with.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Controller Mapping">
    Access the Steam Input menu and select the "Gamepad with Mouse Trackpad" template. This allows the right trackpad to act as a cursor for cabin management tasks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Sensitivity Adjustment">
    Reduce the joystick deadzones in the game settings to ensure responsive handling during bird strikes and engine failures.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Back Button Assignments">
    Map the L4/L5 and R4/R5 back buttons to frequently used actions like "Open Cargo Hold" or "Activate Fire Extinguisher" for faster emergency response.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gyro Aiming for Repairs">
    Enable Gyro for hull repair tasks. When you need to leave the cabin to fix external damage, Gyro allows for more intuitive aiming of repair tools.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The game's physics-driven interactions mean you will often be grabbing and throwing objects. On the Steam Deck, using the triggers for "Grip" and the face buttons for "Interact" creates a natural flow for cabin service.
