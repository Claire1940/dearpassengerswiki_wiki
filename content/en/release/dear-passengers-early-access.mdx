<Section icon={Target} title="Flight Progression: Step-by-Step Beginner Guide">

Completing a flight requires tight cooperation between the cockpit and the cabin. Your primary objective is to deliver your selected loadout safely to the destination while managing escalating mid-air crises.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Passengers and Cargo">
    Before takeoff, review the manifest. High-value cargo and demanding passenger profiles yield higher payouts but introduce extreme flight hazards, such as aggressive passengers or live animal containment risks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assign Cockpit and Cabin Roles">
    Designate one player as the dedicated pilot. The remaining crew members must manage the cabin. Clear division of labor prevents multiple players from abandoning their posts to handle the same emergency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Maintain Flight Path and Speed">
    The pilot must monitor the control instruments, steer through turbulence, and navigate dynamic weather fronts. Smooth handling directly reduces the physics-based chaos happening inside the passenger cabin.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Serve Cabin and Secure Cargo">
    Cabin crew must deliver food and drinks to keep passengers calm. Simultaneously, check on high-risk cargo crates to ensure they remain locked down and do not break open during sharp turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Contain Emergencies and Land">
    Respond immediately to fires, hull breaches, or loose cargo. Once the destination is reached, the crew receives a final payout based on surviving passengers, intact cargo, and overall service quality.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Flight Strategy">
Beginners should start with low-risk passenger and cargo combinations. Do not chase high payouts immediately; mastering basic flight controls and cabin stabilization is essential before taking on high-risk cargo.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="In-Flight Hazards & Emergency Response Protocol">

The core difficulty of a flight stems from unexpected hazards. Physics-based environmental events will throw unsecured players, passengers, and items across the cabin, turning small mistakes into flight-ending disasters.

| Hazard Type | Primary Trigger | First Response Role | Escalation Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dynamic Weather** | Flight path progression | Pilot | Severe turbulence, loss of control, engine damage |
| **Turbulence / Air Pockets** | Storm clouds, sharp turns | Cabin Crew | Loose objects become projectiles, passenger injuries |
| **Onboard Fire** | Electrical failure, accidents | Nearest Crew Member | Spreads rapidly, destroys cargo, damages hull |
| **Escaped Live Cargo** | Unsecured animal crates | Cargo Handler | Blocked aisles, injured passengers, crew distraction |
| **Unruly Passengers** | Poor service, flight delay | Cabin Lead | Passenger panic, service disruption, physical attacks |
| **Hull Damage** | Bird strikes, external attacks | Repair Specialist | Cabin depressurization, engine failure, crash landing |

<Callout type="warning" title="Severe Threat Priority">
If a fire breaks out or live cargo escapes (such as a crocodile), immediately suspend food and beverage services. Unsecured service carts will roll freely during turbulence, blocking pathways and causing extra damage.
</Callout>
