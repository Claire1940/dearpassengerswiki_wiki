<Section icon={Swords} title="Flight Operations and Chaotic In-Flight Mechanics">

At its core, the game challenges a crew of players to keep a highly unstable cargo aircraft airborne. Unlike traditional flight simulators, the cockpit controls are simplified, but the environment surrounding the plane is hostile and unpredictable. Players must manually interact with almost every part of the vehicle, from the steering yoke to the rear cargo ramp. The physical interaction with the plane is highly dynamic; players can open the side doors while flying at high altitudes, exposing the interior cabin to extreme wind forces.

Managing the aircraft is not just about keeping the nose pointed forward. The cabin and exterior wings serve as the active play area. When engines fail or catch fire, players must physically exit the cabin and stand on the wings to perform manual repairs. This exposes them to the risk of falling off, requiring careful movement and timing. Additionally, players must manage volatile cargo, defend the fuselage from boarding parties, and navigate through active combat zones.

| Mechanic | Description | Chaos Rating |
| Wing Standing | Walk outside the plane during flight to fix engines or fight | High |
| Cargo Management | Transporting dangerous items like alligators and heavy crates | Medium |
| Cabin Defense | Defending the interior using weapons like AK-47s against intruders | High |
| Emergency Evacuation | Dealing with sudden engine explosions and mid-air structural failures | Critical |

The introduction of firearms adds a layer of defensive gameplay that is rare in physics-based party simulators. Players can equip weapons like the AK-47 to shoot down intercepting enemy aircraft or to eliminate hostile forces trying to board the cargo hold. This constant transition between maintenance, navigation, and active combat creates a frantic gameplay loop that demands constant attention.
